# M1-L05: The Stewardship Inventory (First Pass)

**Title:** The Stewardship Inventory — First Pass
**Module:** Module 1 — Naming What You Carry
**Duration:** ~2–3 minutes (~360 words)

---

## Narration Script

We are going to do something practical now.

I want you to open a blank document — or a fresh page in a journal, whatever is nearest to hand. This does not need to be beautiful. This does not need to be complete. This is a first pass, which means it is allowed to be rough and honest and alive.

We are going to work through the seven layers together.

Write each layer as a heading. Then, under each one, begin to name what you carry. Don't filter. Don't edit for significance. Don't ask yourself whether it's "really" IP. Just name it.

Layer 1: Lived Experience. What have you lived through that shapes the way you see and do your work? Write it down.

Layer 2: Ancestral and Community Knowledge. What was given to you — by family, by community, by culture — that now lives in how you practice? Name the sources. Name the people, if you can.

Layer 3: Formal Training. Not just the degrees. The deep content expertise. The methodological fluency. What do you know how to do because someone trained you to do it?

Layer 4: Named Frameworks. If you have built anything with a name on it — a protocol, a framework, a model — write it here. Every version, every iteration, even the ones you don't use anymore.

Layer 5: Methodologies and Processes. How do you do what you do? The sequences. The facilitation moves. The diagnostic questions you always ask. The things that work, that you've never written down, because they live in your body.

Layer 6: Teaching and Learning Materials. Every curriculum, workshop, deck, rubric, assessment, or coaching protocol. The finished things and the fragments.

Layer 7: Embodied Wisdom. The knowing that doesn't fit anywhere else. Name it anyway.

Now sit with what you've written.

Notice what surprised you. Notice where the list felt long and where it felt thin. Notice any emotion that rose up — discomfort, pride, grief, recognition.

You do not need to do anything with that yet.

The act of naming is itself the first act of stewardship.

We will build from here.

## B-Roll Scene Descriptions

1. A woman at a desk, writing in a journal. The page is filling with words. Her posture is focused but relaxed — she is not performing. The camera is close enough to feel the intimacy of the act, far enough to give her privacy. The image is: someone meeting themselves on the page.

2. A blank journal page in the foreground, an open window beyond it. The white space of the page is not absence — it is invitation. Morning light. The feeling is beginning, not pressure.

3. A woman mid-pause, pen resting on a page, looking upward in recollection. She is not stuck — she is remembering something. Her face holds a mixture of recognition and surprise, as if finding something she had forgotten was there.

4. An overhead shot of a completed first-draft inventory — multiple layers visible, handwritten, with some items circled and others crossed out. The document is alive. It is a working document, not a finished one. This is what honest first passes look like.

5. A Black woman closing her journal and holding it in both hands for a moment before setting it down. The gesture is one of care — like setting something precious on a shelf. She looks at it for a moment, then looks up. There is something settled in her expression.
