# M1-L01: Stewardship Arrival

**Title:** Stewardship Arrival
**Module:** Module 1 — Naming What You Carry
**Duration:** ~45 seconds

---

## Narration Script

Before we begin, pause.

Close any additional tabs. Silence notifications. Place both feet on the ground.

Take one slow breath in.

And one slow breath out.

You are not here to produce. You are not here to perform. You are here to name something.

And naming takes stillness.

Let your shoulders settle. Let your jaw soften. Let your hands rest.

Bring your attention to what you carry — the knowledge, the frameworks, the experience, the wisdom you have been building, sometimes without even realizing it.

Not what you've published. Not what you've been paid for.

What you actually carry.

Take one more breath.

Inhale.

Exhale.

Now we begin.

## B-Roll Scene Descriptions

1. A Black woman seated at a wide wooden desk, hands resting open on the surface, eyes closed. Warm morning light from a large window behind her. Dust motes visible in the light. The room is quiet, unhurried. Shot from slightly above, wide angle.

2. Close-up of two hands resting flat on a journal, the journal unopened. The hands are still. Outside the frame, daylight. The image holds the feeling of a breath held just before a decision.

3. A woman standing barefoot on a wood floor, feet visible from the knee down. Both feet grounded, deliberate. The rest of the frame is a soft domestic interior — books, plants, natural light. Nothing dramatic. Just presence.

4. A window in a study or home library. On the sill: a single candle, a small stack of books, a pen. The candle is lit. The window looks onto greenery. The shot holds for a moment of pure quiet.

5. A Black woman's face in soft focus, three-quarter profile, eyes downcast in a posture of reflection — not sadness, but deep attention. Warm, diffused light. No text. No action. Just a person inhabiting their own stillness.
